Space Projects Timeline

Discover the key missions in space exploration

2024

Exploration Mission Alpha

An ambitious lunar exploration mission planned for 2024.

This mission aims to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon. It will involve landing astronauts on the lunar surface and developing infrastructure for future missions.

2023

Mars Rover Exploration

A significant Mars Rover mission scheduled for 2023.

This mission will send a new rover to Mars to collect samples and study the planet's surface for signs of past life.

2022

Deep Space Telescope Launch

The launch of the most advanced space telescope occurred in 2022.

The telescope will observe distant galaxies and search for habitable exoplanets using cutting-edge infrared technology.

2021

Asteroid Mining Exploration

Asteroid mining technology was tested in 2021.

This mission tested methods for mining asteroids to extract valuable resources such as rare metals.

2020

Moon Orbiter Mission

A new lunar orbiter was launched in 2020 to map the Moon's surface in greater detail.

This mission enhanced our understanding of the Moon's topography and geology, preparing for future landings.

2019

Space Station Expansion

The International Space Station received new modules and research facilities in 2019.

The expansion included new laboratories, living quarters, and experiments focused on long-duration space travel.

2013

Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission)

India's first interplanetary mission to Mars, providing valuable data.

Launched on November 5, 2013, Mangalyaan entered Martian orbit on September 24, 2014. It continues to provide valuable data about Mars' atmosphere and surface, marking a significant achievement for India's space program.

2008

Chandrayaan-1

India's first lunar probe, which discovered water on the Moon.

Launched on October 22, 2008, Chandrayaan-1 was a significant achievement for ISRO. Although communication was lost in August 2009, the mission successfully detected water molecules on the lunar surface, marking a major discovery in lunar science.

2019

Chandrayaan-2

Ambitious lunar mission with an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan).

Launched on July 22, 2019, Chandrayaan-2's orbiter is still operational and sending data from lunar orbit. The lander Vikram crash-landed, and the rover Pragyan did not deploy. Despite this, the mission has been valuable for lunar exploration.

ISRO's Projects and Missions

1. Satellite Missions

  • GSAT Series: Geostationary satellites for communication (e.g., GSAT-19, GSAT-30).
  • INSAT Series: Indian National Satellite System for meteorology, telecommunication, and broadcasting.
  • IRS Series: Indian Remote Sensing satellites for earth observation.

2. Exploration Missions

  • Chandrayaan-1: India’s first lunar probe, which discovered water on the Moon.
  • Chandrayaan-2: An ambitious lunar exploration mission that included an orbiter, a lander (Vikram), and a rover (Pragyan).
  • Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission): India’s first interplanetary mission to Mars, which has provided significant data on Martian atmosphere and surface.

3. Launch Vehicles

  • PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle): Known for its versatility and success in launching satellites into various orbits.
  • GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle): Used for launching heavier satellites into geostationary orbits.
  • GSLV Mk III: The heaviest launch vehicle, capable of carrying heavier payloads into geosynchronous orbits.

4. Space Research and Development

  • Astrosat: India’s first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory.
  • NavIC: The Navigation with Indian Constellation, India’s own satellite navigation system.

5. Recent Projects

  • Gaganyaan: India’s human spaceflight program aiming to send astronauts to space.
  • Aditya-L1: A mission to study the Sun.
  • Xposat: A space-based observatory to study the high-energy X-ray sources.

Historical Launch Count

As of early 2024, ISRO has conducted over 300 satellite launches and numerous missions across various space exploration areas.
